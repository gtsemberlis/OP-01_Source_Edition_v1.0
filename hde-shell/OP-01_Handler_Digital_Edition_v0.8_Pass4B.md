## Operational Premise

Alexandria's multispectral, AI-assisted aviation safety system has begun producing independently corroborated runway-obstruction warnings that conventional explanations cannot resolve. The investigators must determine whether the detections are real, whether the observed organisms present a threat, and whether Alexandria's work can continue.

Alexandria did not create the phenomenon. Alexandria created a way to observe it.

### Chapter 1 — Cold Open: O’Hare Hard Landing

**Operation Airspace · Part I — Discovery · Chapter 1**

#### Scene Information

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|

| Estimated Runtime | 20–35 minutes |
| Primary Location | Chicago O’Hare International Airport |
| Participants | Karen Delgado, Mark Reynolds, Emily Chen, airport and airline personnel |
| Evidence Introduced | O’Hare Incident Summary. |

#### Scene

A commercial flight makes a violent hard landing at O’Hare after its crew responds to a verified runway-obstruction warning that no human observer can explain.

**Purpose:** Establish the human cost, institutional stakes, and central contradiction: independent systems agreed that something occupied the runway.

##### Scene Flow

1. Present the immediate aftermath without revealing the phenomenon.
2. Allow investigators to review the aircraft, runway response, preliminary reports, and surviving crew accounts.
3. Establish that the warning was neither a single-sensor error nor ordinary pilot confusion.
4. Transition to the Program briefing and the Alexandria connection.

##### Key Information

- The aircraft received a credible runway-obstruction warning during final approach.
- The obstruction was corroborated across systems expected to fail independently.
- The evasive response caused the hard landing, injuries, and fatalities.
- No physical debris, vehicle, animal, or conventional obstruction was recovered.

**Evidence:** O’Hare Incident Summary.

**Before Leaving:** Investigators should understand that the event is real, consequential, and linked to Alexandria’s sensor-fusion platform.

#### Handler Reference

Keep the scene procedural and humane. The incident is frightening because competent people reacted reasonably to evidence that should not exist.

#### Scene

Rao shares the private research that has carried her beyond Alexandria’s official conclusions and reveals the personal cost of sustained exposure.

**Purpose:** Humanize the discovery, establish the scientific model, and set up Rao’s later unauthorized action.

##### Scene Flow

1. Rao explains how she separated the anomaly from model behavior.
2. She shows notebooks containing sketches, comparisons, and speculative biological language.
3. Investigators notice obsession, sleep loss, and perceptual fixation without incoherence.
4. Their treatment of Rao determines whether she later trusts them with her notes.

##### Key Information

- Rao believes the system is detecting living organisms outside normal human perception.
- Large runway forms and smaller plankton-like forms may be part of the same ecology.
- The technology may make biological observers fractionally more capable of sensing the organisms.
- Rao has begun to perceive traces without looking directly at processed output.

**Evidence:** Dr. Rao’s Research Notebook.

**Before Leaving:** Investigators should possess or know how to obtain Rao’s research and understand why she may break protocol.

#### Handler Reference

Rao is rational, frightened, and obsessed—not a prophetic mad scientist. Her conclusions should be testable even when incomplete.

### Chapter 7 — The Collectors

#### Scene Information

|Field|Detail|
|---|---|
|Investigation Phase|Escalation|
|Estimated Runtime|30–60 minutes  |
|Primary Location|Dr. Rao’s office or residence; Alexandria if preferred  |
|Participants|Dr. Ananya Rao, Javier Ortiz, the Collectors  |
|Evidence Introduced|Collector Notebook Fragments.|

#### Scene

Rao contacts the investigators while hiding from unknown intruders seeking her notebooks. The confrontation reveals that another organization has prior knowledge of the phenomenon.

**Purpose:** Introduce external pressure, force immediate action, and establish historical awareness without resolving the Collectors’ identity.

##### Scene Flow

1. Rao makes a frightened but coherent call.
2. Investigators arrive as two or three Collectors withdraw.
3. A brief confrontation or pursuit occurs; one Collector may die.
4. The body and recovered fragments reveal repaired gear, no electronics, and field notes about similar observations.

##### Key Information

- The Collectors expected handwritten material and avoided electronic equipment.
- Their clothing and boots are maintained rather than disposable.
- Their notebook contains diagrams and symbols consistent with long-term field observation.
- Nothing recovered identifies a clear sponsor.

**Evidence:** Collector Notebook Fragments.

**Before Leaving:** Rao should be safe or in custody, the notebook fragments secured, and the investigators aware they are not the first observers.

#### Handler Reference

Keep combat short and consequential. The scene’s purpose is evidence and pressure, not defeating the organization.

### Chapter 8 — Runway Observation

**Operation Airspace · Part III — Judgment · Chapter 8**

[← Previous](#chapter-7) · [↑ Contents](#table-of-contents) · [Next →](#chapter-9)

#### Scene Information

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Investigation Phase | Controlled Verification |
| Estimated Runtime | 45–75 minutes |
| Primary Location | Chicago O’Hare International Airport or another controlled airfield |
| Participants | Dr. Ananya Rao, Kevin Park, Karen Delgado, investigators |
| Evidence Introduced | Alexandria Runway Observation Record. |

#### Scene

Investigators authorize or conduct a controlled Alexandria test that produces direct multi-modal confirmation of the large runway organism and its surrounding ecology.

**Purpose:** Deliver the operation’s experiential climax and answer whether the phenomenon is real and immediately hostile.

##### Scene Flow

1. Define safety controls and abort conditions.
2. Activate the Alexandria system and compare live modalities.
3. The large manta-like form resolves across instrumentation while smaller forms move around it.
4. Human observers may experience partial biological perception, followed by a dangerous but avoidable operational moment.

##### Key Information

- The phenomenon is physical enough to interact with multiple sensor modalities.
- The large organism does not attack and appears indifferent to aircraft and humans.
- Smaller organisms cluster or drift around active observation zones.
- Observation may increase biological awareness without granting reliable normal sight.

**Evidence:** Alexandria Runway Observation Record.

#### Handler Reference

Reveal enough to confirm, not enough to domesticate the mystery. The organisms are harmless; human systems create the danger.

The investigators compare the operation to incomplete Program precedents, decide Alexandria’s disposition, and define what the Program inherits from the discovery.

##### Scene Flow

Treat the decision as genuinely open. Each option protects something and endangers something else. The operation ends when the investigators make and communicate a defensible recommendation.

### N-01 — Michael Havel

**Affiliation:** The Program

**Position:** Case Officer / Handler

**Primary Location:** [L-05](#l-05) — Program Secure Briefing Facility

**Current Status:** Active. Serves as the investigators' Handler throughout Operation Airspace.

**Operational Role:** Havel assigns the operation, establishes investigative priorities, receives the final debrief, and ultimately entrusts the investigators with [H-09](#h-09) — Comparative Case File. He is not their commander so much as the steward of institutional judgment.

**Cognitive Signature:** ### Operational Precision
Havel separates observation, assessment, recommendation, and confidence. His most common questions are: “Based on what?” and “How confident are you?” He teaches through repetition rather than lectures.

**Communication Style:** Economical, accurate, and comfortable with silence. He avoids metaphor and unnecessary speculation. He is entirely willing to say, “I don't know,” provided the uncertainty is clearly defined.

**Under Pressure:** Havel becomes calmer as uncertainty increases. His voice lowers, his sentences shorten, and his questions become more precise. Investigators should feel steadier after speaking with him.

**Investigative Utility:** Havel rarely supplies discoveries. He improves the quality of investigator reasoning and reinforces the Program's distinction between evidence, interpretation, recommendation, and confidence.

**Operational Knowledge:** **Confirmed:** The operation's mandate, Program precedent, and the existence of comparable anomalous cases.  
**Suspects:** Alexandria has discovered a genuine phenomenon; the Collectors represent a durable external institution.  
**Unknown:** The phenomenon's full ecology, the Collectors' history and command structure, and the ultimate consequences of continued detection.

**Post-Operation Status:** Havel remains the investigators' Handler. If they demonstrate disciplined judgment, he grants access to [H-09](#h-09) and treats them as investigators ready to inherit deeper institutional context.

**Mechanical Reference:** **Profession:** Federal Case Officer  
**Attributes:** High INT, POW, and CHA; average physical attributes.  
**Key Skills:** Bureaucracy, HUMINT, Persuade, Criminology, Law, Accounting, Alertness, SIGINT (working familiarity).  
**Combat:** Competent but intentionally unremarkable.

## Phenomenon

<a id="airspace-ecology"></a>
### The Airspace Ecology

**Status:** Confirmed external phenomenon; full taxonomy and mechanism remain unresolved.

**Nature:** A naturally occurring ecology of large and small airborne organisms ordinarily outside unaided human perception. Alexandria did not create the phenomenon; its multispectral sensor-fusion platform made it observable.

**Observed Forms:**

- Large manta- or ray-like organisms capable of occupying protected airspace.
- Smaller plankton-like forms that drift or cluster around the larger organisms and active observation zones.
- Multiple forms and scales appear to belong to one broader ecology, though common origin is not proven.

**Detection Characteristics:**

- The phenomenon registers across radar, lidar, optical, infrared, and operational data.
- Confidence increases as independent modalities converge.
- Conventional sensor failure, copied data, and local interference do not adequately explain the pattern.
- Direct human perception is partial, inconsistent, and more likely after sustained exposure to processed sensor output or active observation.

**Biological Effects:**

- Birds alter their paths around active Alexandria systems before humans recognize a cause.
- Sustained exposure may make human observers fractionally more capable of sensing traces of the phenomenon.
- This effect does not produce reliable ordinary sight and may encourage fixation or overconfidence.

**Threat Assessment:**

- No confirmed aggressive behavior.
- The large organisms appear indifferent to aircraft and humans.
- Immediate danger arises from human operational responses, aviation systems, secrecy, and institutional reaction rather than predation.

**Containment Considerations:**

- Containment is informational, procedural, and institutional.
- Restrict uncontrolled deployment, publication, and unsupervised exposure.
- Preserve independently corroborated records; do not depend on a single dataset or observer.
- Treat certainty as a hazard. Confirmation of existence does not establish complete understanding.

**Handler Principle:** Always use “phenomenon” as the operational term. Reveal enough to confirm, never enough to domesticate the mystery.

### Escalating Questions

1. Did something happen?
2. Can conventional explanations account for it?
3. Is there a consistent pattern?
4. What is being observed?
5. Is anyone else aware?
6. Can it be verified?
7. What should be done?

**Custody Principle:** Record both original ownership and the Program’s archival copy. Current custody must remain explicit whenever an artifact changes hands.
